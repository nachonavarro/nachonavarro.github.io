About

Intro

I'm a fourth year student pursuing a Masters Engineering at Imperial College London, studying Joint Mathematics and Computer Science. I was born in Valencia, Spain, although I've been living here and there for most part of my life. I spent a year in 2000 (when I was 4!) in Madison, Wisconsin, where my father taught Group Theory at UW. I then spent a year in 2004 in Gainesville, Florida (University of Florida), 6 months in UC Berkeley, California in 2008, and finally another 6 months again at UW in 2010. I moved to London, U.K. in the summer of 2014 to start my studies at Imperial.

Here are the six schools I've attended:

  • Preschool Lab UW, Madison
  • Santa Ana, Valencia
  • American School of Valencia, Valencia
  • Littlewood Elementary, Gainesville
  • Piedmont Middle School, Piedmont (Oakland)
  • West High School, Madison

Interests

My main interests lie in coding theory and in cryptography. Some topics that interest me include homomorphic encryption and the importance of pseudorandomness on cryptography. However, in general I'm just interested in fields of computer science where math plays an important role.

In the summer of 2017 due mostly to my internship at J.P. Morgan I became very interested in data science (in particular anomaly detection), time series analysis, and Big Data.
